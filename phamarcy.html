PHAMARCY

Pharmacy in a hospital plays a critical role in patient care. The hospital pharmacy is responsible for ensuring that medications are safely and accurately dispensed to patients.

Some of the key functions of a hospital pharmacy include:
1 . Medication procurement: The hospital pharmacy is responsible for procuring medications from approved sources and maintaining an adequate inventory.
2 . Medication storage: The pharmacy ensures that medications are stored in a safe and secure environment, and that they are properly labeled and tracked....
3 . Medication dispensing: The pharmacy dispenses medications to patients according to physician orders and in a timely manner.
4 . Medication monitoring: The pharmacy monitors patients' medication therapy to ensure that the medications are being used safely and effectively, and that any adverse reactions or interactions are identified and addressed.
5 . Patient education: The pharmacy provides education to patients and their families about their medications, including dosing, administration, side effects, and drug interactions.
6 . Collaborating with healthcare providers: The pharmacy collaborates with physicians, nurses, and other healthcare providers to ensure that patients receive appropriate medications and that their medication therapy is optimized.
Overall, the hospital pharmacy plays a crucial role in ensuring that patients receive safe and effective medication therapy.
